Peppermint fudge

Something new to try this holiday season

While it would be natural for a family to make a batch of gingerbread men, or even order a cheesecake, in some families it's a tradition to make a batch of fudge, either for gifts or for desert. Here I'll go over the process for making a candy cane fudge, starting with the ingredients.

  • 20 ounces of vanilla baking chips
  • 14 ounces of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon of peppermint extract
  • 1 and 1/2 cups of crushed candy cane
  • a dash of green or red food coloring
  1. line an 8 inch baking pan with greased aluminum foil
  2. Combine the condensed milk and vanilla chips into a saucepan over medium heat
  3. Stir untill almost melted, then remove from heat and continue mixing till smooth
  4. When completely melted, mix in the food coloring, peppermint extract, and top with candy canes
  5. Spread mixture into the baking pan, and chill for 2 hours
